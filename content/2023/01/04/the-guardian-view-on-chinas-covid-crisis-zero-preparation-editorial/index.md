China’s citizens know this winter is a deadly one. But they do not know how deadly. According to leaked information, health officials [estimated](https://www.ft.com/content/1fb6044a-3050-44d8-b715-80c18ca5c9ab) that about 250 million people – 18% of the population – were infected with Covid in the first 20 days of December and that as of Christmas Eve, the rate of infection was still rising.

C 中国公民知道今年冬天是致命的。但他们不知道有多致命。根据泄露的信息，卫生官员估计，在12月的前20天，大约有2.5亿人——占人口的18%——感染了新冠病毒，截至平安夜，感染率仍在上升。

Yet the authorities’ public pronouncements have been extremely limited and clearly misleading or downright ludicrous. The virus rips through the country and bodies [wait days for collection](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-01-03/bodies-pile-up-in-china-as-covid-surge-overwhelms-crematoriums) – but on Monday, China reported 76 Covid deaths, of which all but two were in Hong Kong, not on the mainland. Relatives report loved ones dying of Covid but having something else [recorded on the death certificate](https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2022/12/china-zero-covid-policy-end-vaccine-rates-deaths/672596/). Worse may be to come: while there are signs of younger people recovering in Beijing and Shanghai, the lunar new year is fast approaching, and with it the return of hundreds of millions of migrants, on planes, crowded trains and buses, to smaller towns and rural areas with far worse healthcare than big cities.

然而，当局的公开声明极其有限，而且明显具有误导性或彻头彻尾的荒唐可笑。这种病毒席卷全国，尸体等待数天才能收集到——但周一，中国报告了76例新冠死亡病例，其中除两人外都在香港，而不是大陆。亲属报告亲人死于新冠，但死亡证明上有其他记录。更糟糕的情况可能还会出现：尽管北京和上海有年轻人康复的迹象，但农历新年即将到来，数亿农民工也将随之返回，他们乘坐飞机、拥挤的火车和公共汽车，回到医疗保健比大城市差得多的小城镇和农村地区。

The government’s U-turn probably reflected concern about the economic impact (which the International Monetary Fund predicts will grow [at or below global growth](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2023/jan/02/third-of-world-economy-to-hit-recession-in-2023-imf-head-warns) for the first time in decades), public discontent with zero Covid and the realisation that infections were surging despite its harsh rules. Its big mistake was neither initially pursuing a zero-Covid policy nor lifting it – others did so and kept mortality rates low – but shifting without warning, much mitigation or, apparently, preparation.

政府的转变可能反映了对经济影响的担忧（国际货币基金组织预测，全球经济增长率将在几十年来首次达到或低于全球增长率）、公众对新冠疫情为零的不满，以及意识到尽管其规定严厉，感染人数仍在激增。它的大错误既不是最初奉行零新冠政策，也不是取消政策——其他国家这样做了，并将死亡率保持在低水平——而是在没有警告、大幅缓解或显然准备的情况下转变。

Other governments, including the US and the UK, have been largely forgiven by many of their citizens for deadly errors in their pandemic response. Chinese authorities are expert in rewriting history. Yet at least some citizens are radically reassessing their assumptions about the party-state and “authoritarian competence”. The reckless unlocking has made them more bitter about the apparently pointless restrictions preceding it. For many of the younger generation, this is their [first experience](https://www.ft.com/content/8205e62c-10a8-450a-a87a-ddcf8f6eeca3) of real hard times; for their elders, it may recall previous party follies. Xi Jinping has not only made it clear that he calls the shots in China, he was also [directly and personally associated](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/jan/01/chinas-president-xi-battles-to-save-face-as-covid-u-turn-weakens-his-grip-on-power) with the draconian measures. The Chinese leader struck an atypical note in his new year address when he remarked: “Ours is a big country. It is only natural for different people to have different concerns or hold different views on the same issue.”

包括美国和英国在内的其他国家政府，由于在应对疫情时犯了致命错误，基本上得到了许多公民的原谅。中国当局擅长改写历史。然而，至少一些公民正在彻底重新评估他们对党的国家和“威权能力”的假设。鲁莽的解锁让他们对之前明显毫无意义的限制更加不满。对许多年轻一代来说，这是他们第一次经历真正的艰难时期；对他们的长辈来说，这可能会让他们想起党以前的愚蠢行为。习近平不仅明确表示，他在中国发号施令，他还直接和个人与严厉措施有关联。这位中国领导人在新年致辞中用了一个不寻常的语气，他说：“我们是一个大国。不同的人对同一个问题有不同的关切或不同的看法是很自然的。”

It is absurd for Beijing to attack testing requirements imposed on inbound passengers by other nations; it still insists on a negative PCR test to enter the country, and is patently failing to offer full and accurate information on the pandemic’s progress. Sampling of passengers and wastewater from planes is [sensible](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2023/jan/03/china-is-now-the-epicentre-of-covid-the-world-should-be-watching-and-testing) so that potentially dangerous new variants can be identified and monitored.

中国政府攻击其他国家对入境旅客实施的检测要求是荒谬的。中国政府仍坚持要求入境旅客接受阴性PCR检测，而且显然未能提供有关疫情进展的全面准确信息。对乘客和飞机废水进行采样是明智的，这样就可以识别和监测潜在危险的新变种。

But for countries that have otherwise [essentially given up on Covid-reduction measures](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2022/dec/31/sunaks-u-turn-on-china-covid-tests-is-a-political-move-say-scientists) (unlike, say, Japan), and which seem largely unconcerned about the known XBB.1.5 variant from the US, to insist on negative PCR tests from all passengers seems largely performative. The EU offer of free vaccines – though rejected by Beijing – was a more useful response. Given the [disturbing increase in anti-Asian hate and violence](https://www.healthaffairs.org/do/10.1377/forefront.20220411.655787/) since the pandemic began, politicians must not promote further prejudice that could endanger those of east and south-east Asian origin or heritage, and Chinese citizens who have already suffered at their own government’s hands.

但对于那些基本上已经放弃新冠减少措施的国家（比如日本），以及那些似乎基本上不关心已知的美国XBB.1.5变种的国家来说，坚持所有乘客的聚合酶链反应检测呈阴性似乎在很大程度上是行之有效的。欧盟提供免费疫苗的提议——尽管遭到北京方面的拒绝——是一个更有用的回应。鉴于自疫情爆发以来反亚洲仇恨和暴力的令人不安的增加，政客们不能助长可能危及东亚和东南亚血统或传统的人，以及已经在自己政府手中受苦的中国公民的进一步偏见。
